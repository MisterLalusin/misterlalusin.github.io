John Rovic Dusdus Lalusin is a professional skilled in software engineering, computer engineering, design engineering, artificial intellegence, machine learning, IoT, and embedded systems. He earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Engineering with a specialization in Robotics Systems from STI Academic Center Lipa. Notably, he was honored as the Design Engineer of The Year and received recognition for Best Capstone Design, all while graduating with Cum Laude in 2023.

Despite not pursuing a STEM track in Senior High School, his foundation in TechVoc-ICT (IT in Mobile App and Web Development) granted him a valuable edge, emphasizing practical skills over theoretical knowledge. Furthermore, Mr. Lalusin was a key member of the only Grade 11 team to reach the STI Tagisan ng Talino Codefest National Level in 2018, securing the first-runner-up position despite competing against more experienced tertiary students.

His achievements extend to national-level recognition as a finalist and a top 4 finish in the Smart Property category at the inaugural PacketHacks IoT Technology Hackathon held at the World Trade Center Metro Manila. Here, Mr. Lalusin's team competed against a diverse field of contestants, including Microsoft interns, showcasing their designated IoT devices. This competition was evaluated by experts from both local and international companies.

Impressively, even before graduating from college, Mr. Lalusin had already accumulated four years of professional experience, showcasing his versatility and adaptability across various technological domains; in addition to Computer Engineering, he contemplates pursuing other engineering programs, particularly Electrical Engineering and Mechanical Engineering, in the future, driven by his unquenchable thirst for knowledge and passion for machines, while also actively seeking opportunities to further enhance his skill set and contribute to cutting-edge innovations. Moreover, at the young age of 22, he initiated his stock investing journey and become a retail investor, diligently setting aside a considerable portion of his earnings to invest in companies specializing in research and development, the energy sector, and the petroleum industry particularly focusing on stocks associated with technological advancements.

Professional Career

Mr. Lalusin embarked on his professional journey by collaborating with burgeoning tech enterprises. Shortly after completing his senior high school education, he assumed the role of IT Head at Outsourcing Solution PH, a tech startup. In this capacity, he oversaw a team of recent college graduates who specialized in crafting software solutions for government entities, with a particular emphasis on mobile application development. Concurrently, he joined ARC System Solutions Co, another startup dedicated to providing software solutions for healthcare facilities in Batangas province. Following this, he joined a Japanese IT firm situated in Eastwood Quezon City, all the while completing his final semester of studies. In this new environment, he primarily focused on backend technologies, contributing to the development of web-based systems for Japanese businesses. His swift progression continued as he secured a mid-level software engineer position at one of the Philippines' leading ride-hailing and delivery superapps, showcasing his dedication and expertise in the competitive tech industry.

Engineering Thesis

Sensor-based Integrated Emergency Alert System Using Raspberry Pi - BSCpE (STI Academic Center Lipa)

Prototype

Nearly a century has elapsed since the inception of closed-circuit television (CCTV). Over time, it has undergone various adaptations, yet a fundamental constraint endures: the need for constant 24/7 human surveillance. This limitation has led to a recurring issue, particularly in the Philippines, where emergencies have been overlooked, resulting in responders arriving only after the events have concluded. What if there existed a technology capable of autonomously alerting responders? What if, instead of relying on CCTV cameras, communities employed audio processing technologies to detect emergency situations? Such a system could be deployed in banks, actively listening for keywords like "holdup" to trigger immediate emergency alerts. Similarly, public transportation could benefit from this technology, given that holdups are common in urban areas during late hours when response times are extended. Developers have also introduced a mobile version that offers analogous functionality, listening for emergencies and serving as a personal bodyguard, even in low-light conditions. RPIEAS and the EarTags system rely on voice recognition, enabling users to summon assistance even when incapacitated or deprived of sight.
