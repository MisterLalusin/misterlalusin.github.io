Hackaton

PacketHacks IoT Technology Hackaton 2023 - World Trade Center Metro Manila

Lalusin at PacketHacks IoT Technology Hackaton 2023

In 2023, while Mr. Lalusin was diligently working in Quezon City, he received an invitation from Mr. Justine Louis Sacdalan to join forces with Mr. Emmanuel David Arganda and Alyca Villalon, who was stepping in as a replacement for Ms. Julie Ann Mirabel, for one final codefest journey. However, their dreams of reaching the finals were dashed when the committee enforced stricter rules, barring graduating students from the competition.

Fearing that his last year as an STIer would be uneventful, a glimmer of hope emerged when Engr. Mary Ann Macatangay encouraged them to submit their thesis for the upcoming PacketHacks IoT Technology Hackathon, scheduled to take place at the World Trade Center Metro Manila. On June 3, 2023, just moments before their Final Thesis Defense, Mr. Lalusin received an email informing them that they had secured a spot as one of the finalists in the IoT Hackathon, ranking among the top 4 in the Smart Property category.

This development provided Mr. Lalusin and his team members, Carlo Castro, Angelo Jose Nulial, and Vince Warren Oñate, with an opportunity to demonstrate their prowess as Computer Engineers. The competition spanned from June 26 to 28, but due to financial constraints, the team could only attend the last day. Fortunately, June 28 coincided with a holiday, allowing Mr. Lalusin to participate in the demo day without compromising his role as a software engineer.

On the final day, they showcased their innovative product, competing against Microsoft interns in their category, with judges hailing from both local and international companies. While they didn't clinch victory in their designated category, they left a lasting impression on the judges. One foreign judge even urged them to consider patenting their idea, recognizing the risk of larger US companies replicating it. Another judge commended their audacious approach and expressed hope that future generations would continue to push the boundaries of innovation.

STI Tagisan ng Talino Codefest 2018 - National Level

Lalusin at STI TNT Codefest 2018

In 2018, Mr. Lalusin embarked on his Codefest journey alongside Mr. Emmanuel David Arganda and Julie Ann Mirabel, representing ICT-102, a grade 11 section at STI College Lipa. Despite facing more seasoned tertiary students, they miraculously advanced in the local competition. Justine Louis Sacdalan, an STI Balayan alumnus, served as their coach. They later competed in the cluster competition at STI Calamba, encountering even tougher opponents that led to a tiebreaker in their favor.

Their journey continued as they represented Cluster 4 at the national level, hosted at STI Ortigas-Cainta. This national competition involved a 72-hour hackathon to devise a business solution based on a provided case. They emerged as the 1st Runner Up that year, earning the opportunity to witness other national competitions held at STI Vigan.

The team regrouped the following year but faced heartbreak after falling short in the Cluster 4 competition at STI Batangas, securing only the 2nd Runner Up position. This marked their final journey together as a team, as Ms. Julie Ann Mirabel opted to attend a different school for college.

In 2020, Mr. Lalusin returned to Codefest with new members, Jose Jerome Lalunio and Kaye Angel Lanto, under the guidance of their coach, Mr. James Olependo. They sought redemption by defeating STI Las Piñas on their home turf and clinching the 1st Runner Up title in the Cluster 4 Codefest Competition. Unfortunately, this marked the conclusion of his Codefest career, as the pandemic forced the cancellation of the competition for the next two years.


Engineering Thesis

Sensor-based Integrated Emergency Alert System Using Raspberry Pi

Prototype

Nearly a century has elapsed since the inception of closed-circuit television (CCTV). Over time, it has undergone various adaptations, yet a fundamental constraint endures: the need for constant 24/7 human surveillance. This limitation has led to a recurring issue, particularly in the Philippines, where emergencies have been overlooked, resulting in responders arriving only after the events have concluded. What if there existed a technology capable of autonomously alerting responders? What if, instead of relying on CCTV cameras, communities employed audio processing technologies to detect emergency situations? Such a system could be deployed in banks, actively listening for keywords like "holdup" to trigger immediate emergency alerts. Similarly, public transportation could benefit from this technology, given that holdups are common in urban areas during late hours when response times are extended. Developers have also introduced a mobile version that offers analogous functionality, listening for emergencies and serving as a personal bodyguard, even in low-light conditions. RPIEAS and the EarTags system rely on voice recognition, enabling users to summon assistance even when incapacitated or deprived of sight.
